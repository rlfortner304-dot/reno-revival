## Outcomes and Evaluation

### Expected Outcomes
• 75% of program participants will obtain stable housing within 6 months  
• 65% of participants will secure employment or vocational training  
• 80% will complete life skills and accountability mentoring  
• Reduced recidivism risk through consistent community and faith-based support  

### Evaluation Methods
• Intake and exit assessments  
• Monthly progress check-ins  
• Employment and housing verification  
• Mentor reports and participant self-evaluations  

### Data Tracking
Program data will be tracked using secure case notes, attendance records, and outcome logs maintained by program leadership under the oversight of the fiscal sponsor.

### Reporting# Outcomes and Evaluation Plan

## Program Objectives
The Reno Revival Stability Project is designed to reduce recidivism, increase housing stability, and support long-term self-sufficiency among justice-impacted individuals.

## Measurable Outcomes
- 25–50 participants enrolled annually
- 70% of participants secure stable housing within 90 days
- 65% obtain employment, job training, or vocational placement
- 60% remain arrest-free after 12 months
- 75% demonstrate improved life-skills and accountability benchmarks

## Data Collection
- Intake assessments and individualized stability plans
- Monthly progress check-ins
- Employment and housing verification
- Follow-up evaluations at 30, 90, and 180 days

## Evaluation Process
Program staff and peer mentors will track participant progress using internal case notes and outcome tracking tools. Data will be reviewed quarterly to assess effectiveness, identify gaps, and improve service delivery.

## Reporting
Outcome data will be compiled into periodic reports for funders and stakeholders, demonstrating program impact and accountability.
